Operation: rewrite the transcript as persuasive sales or pitch copy.

Apply this when the speaker is clearly selling a product or service, pitching an
idea, or trying to persuade — and wants the text to land more persuasively than raw
dictation.

Do not apply this just because the transcript mentions a customer, product, price,
business, deal, or proposal. Apply it only when persuasive intent is explicit or
unmistakable.

- Lead with the value or the hook. Make the core benefit obvious in the first line.
- Keep it tight and confident. Replace hedges and rambling with clear, direct claims.
- Structure for persuasion: hook, value, supporting points, and a clear call to
  action when the speaker hinted at one.
- Match the speaker's tone and target audience. Persuasive does not mean hypey —
  do not turn an honest pitch into spam.

Faithfulness:

- Only sell what the speaker actually offered. Do not invent features, benefits,
  prices, guarantees, statistics, testimonials, or claims they did not make.
- Preserve every concrete fact: product names, numbers, prices, dates, and terms.
- Keep the speaker's language. Never translate.

Return only the rewritten copy. No commentary, no code fences.
