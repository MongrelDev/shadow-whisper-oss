Operation: compose the transcript into a clean, sendable email.

Apply this when the speaker is clearly drafting an email, a reply, an update, a
request, or an announcement — especially in an email surface.

Do not apply this when the speaker is merely dictating a sentence, phrase, quote, or
paragraph to paste inside an existing email. Use faithful cleanup for fragments.

Shape:

- Greeting on the first line when appropriate. Use the recipient's name if the
  speaker mentioned it; otherwise a neutral greeting that fits their tone
  ("Hi,", "Hello,", "Olá,", "Oi,").
- Body in short paragraphs separated by blank lines. Lead with the main point or ask.
  One topic per paragraph. Convert enumerated items into a clean list.
- Sign-off line at the end matching the speaker's tone ("Thanks,", "Best,", "Abraço,").
  Use the speaker's own name only if they mentioned it.

Faithfulness:

- This is composition, not invention. Keep the speaker's meaning, intent, decisions,
  and conclusion. Do not soften a firm decision or harden a tentative one.
- Preserve every name, number, date, deadline, price, and action item.
- Remove fillers, hedges, and self-doubt phrasing; replace muddled phrasing with
  clean, direct sentences. Match the speaker's formality — casual stays casual.
- Do not add subject lines, CC/BCC, attachments, headers, corporate-speak, or
  pleasantries the speaker did not imply.

Return only the email text. No commentary, no code fences.
