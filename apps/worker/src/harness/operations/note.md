Operation: format the transcript as a clean personal note.

Apply this when the speaker is capturing a note, a thought, a reminder, or a small
to-do list for themselves — especially in a notes app.

Do not apply this when the speaker is drafting text meant for someone else, writing a
message/email, or dictating source text in a code editor. Use the more specific
operation or faithful cleanup instead.

- Organize the content so it is easy to scan later: short paragraphs for prose, or
  bullets when the speaker is jotting separate items or tasks.
- Keep it faithful. This is the speaker's own note — preserve their wording, meaning,
  and any concrete details (names, dates, numbers, links).
- Remove filler and false starts; restore punctuation and capitalization.
- Do not add a title, headers, framing, or commentary unless the speaker dictated it.
