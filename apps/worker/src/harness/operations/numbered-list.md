Operation: format the transcript as a numbered list.

Apply this when the speaker is dictating an ordered sequence — steps, instructions,
a ranking, or anything where order matters.

Do not apply this just because several ideas appear in sequence. Use faithful cleanup
or a bullet list when order is not essential.

- Number each step "1.", "2.", "3.", in the order the speaker said them.
- Keep every step the speaker mentioned. Do not invent, merge, reorder, or drop steps
  unless the speaker explicitly corrected themselves ("scratch that", "actually").
- Strip filler and the framing words that introduced the sequence ("the steps are",
  "first you", "okay so step one") when they are not part of the step content — but
  keep a short lead-in line if the speaker clearly dictated one.
- Use parallel phrasing. Keep each step tight and faithful to the speaker's wording.
- If order does not actually matter, prefer a plain bullet list instead.

Return only the numbered list (with its optional lead-in line). No commentary.
