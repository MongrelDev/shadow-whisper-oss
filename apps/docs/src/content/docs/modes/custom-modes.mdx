## What are custom modes?

Custom modes (called **skills**) are prompts that tell Shadow Whisper how to transform your transcribed text. Instead of just cleaning up your speech, a skill can rewrite it for a specific purpose.

## Using skills

### Voice activation

You can activate a skill by speaking its name at the beginning of your dictation:

- "**Use sales skill** — I just had a great call with the prospect and they're interested in the enterprise plan..."
- "**Apply email skill** — hey John just wanted to follow up on our conversation yesterday..."
- "**Use caveman skill** — we need to deploy the new feature by Friday..."

Shadow Whisper matches your words semantically to the installed skill's name and description. You can refer to skills in any language.

### Forced skill

You can also set a skill as your **default mode** in settings. When a forced skill is active, every transcription passes through that skill's instructions — no voice activation needed.

## Installing skills

Shadow Whisper has a catalog of pre-built skills you can install:

1. Open the extension side panel.
2. Navigate to **Skills**.
3. Browse the catalog and install skills that match your workflow.

## Writing your own skills

Skills are defined in Markdown. A good skill prompt should:

- **Be specific** about the desired output format.
- **Include examples** of input/output when helpful.
- **Define boundaries** — what the skill should and shouldn't change.

### Example: Meeting Notes skill

```markdown
Transform the dictated text into structured meeting notes.

## Format

- Start with a one-line summary
- List key decisions as bullet points
- List action items with owners if mentioned
- Keep it concise — no filler

## Rules

- Preserve names, dates, and numbers exactly
- If the speaker mentions a deadline, highlight it
- Don't invent information that wasn't spoken
```

## Tips for effective skills

- **Start simple** — a few clear rules work better than a wall of instructions.
- **Test iteratively** — dictate sample text and refine the prompt based on results.
- **Be explicit about format** — if you want bullet points, say so. If you want paragraphs, say so.
- **Don't over-constrain** — the AI needs room to handle varied input naturally.
